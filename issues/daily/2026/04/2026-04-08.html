Anthropic's Mythos security model finds thousands of vulnerabilities but is restricted to 40 organizations due to its offensive capabilities

Anthropic released Mythos Preview only to 40 handpicked organizations including AWS, Google, Microsoft, and Apple because the model's autonomous vulnerability discovery capabilities far exceed what can be safely deployed publicly. In testing, Mythos found a 27-year-old OpenBSD vulnerability, chained Linux kernel flaws for complete system takeover, and achieved an 83.1% first-attempt exploit success rate. Anthropic also locked in 3.5 gigawatts of compute capacity for scaling operations.

What matters today

ANTHROPIC MYTHOS: RESTRICTED RELEASE

Anthropic withheld its Mythos security model from public release, granting access only to 40 organizations due to capabilities that far exceed any previous model in autonomous vulnerability discovery and exploitation.

  • Mythos Preview shared with 40 orgs only: AWS, Google, Microsoft, Apple and 36 others in security and infrastructure.
  • Found a vulnerability in OpenBSD that had survived 27 years of human review and millions of automated scans.
  • Autonomously chained multiple Linux kernel flaws to achieve complete system takeover in testing.
  • 83.1% first-attempt exploit success rate across a diverse test suite of real-world vulnerabilities.
  • Exceeds Claude Opus 4.6's capability (approximately 500 zero-days found) by an order of magnitude.
  • Raises the question of whether any restricted-access distribution is truly contained at this capability level.

ANTHROPIC: 3.5GW COMPUTE

Anthropic secured 3.5 gigawatts of compute capacity, a significant infrastructure commitment that signals confidence in sustained demand growth for its model portfolio.

  • 3.5GW is a substantial data center commitment; comparable deals typically take 12-18 months to come online.
  • Comes alongside the Mythos reveal and agent platform restrictions: Anthropic is investing heavily in capability while tightening distribution.
  • Positions Anthropic to compete with OpenAI and Google on infrastructure scale through 2027-2028.
