April 9, 2026 Wednesday edition

Meta Superintelligence Labs ships Muse Spark, its first model, with a multi-agent deliberation mode built in

Meta's new Superintelligence Labs unit shipped its first model on Wednesday. Muse Spark handles voice, text, and image, and introduces a "contemplating mode" that routes queries through a multi-agent deliberation loop before responding. Meanwhile OpenAI mapped out a path to $100B in ad revenue by 2030, and HeyGen cracked the identity-drift problem that has plagued AI avatar video for years.

What matters today

META SUPERINTELLIGENCE LABS

Meta Superintelligence Labs ships Muse Spark, a multimodal model (voice, text, image) with a "contemplating mode" that uses multi-agent deliberation to reason before responding.

  • Muse Spark is the first public release from Meta Superintelligence Labs, the unit Zuckerberg stood up to consolidate Meta's frontier AI bets.
  • Contemplating mode routes the query through a network of specialized agents that deliberate and reconcile before a final response is generated, trading latency for reasoning depth.
  • Multimodal from the start: voice input, text, and image handling unified in one model rather than bolted on later.
  • Positions Meta ahead of the next platform cycle, where multi-agent architectures are expected to replace single-model responses for complex tasks.

OPENAI: $100B ADS BY 2030

OpenAI projects $100B in advertising revenue by 2030, with $2.5B targeted for 2026. An internal ad pilot already generated $100M in ARR in under two months.

  • $100M ARR in fewer than 60 days from a pilot that was not publicly announced, suggesting strong advertiser demand for AI-native placement.
  • $2.5B ad revenue projected for 2026 alone, setting up ads as a second major revenue pillar alongside API and ChatGPT subscriptions.
  • Marks a significant strategic shift: OpenAI is building a media business, not just an API business, as it scales toward profitability.

HEYGEN AVATAR V

HeyGen launches Avatar V, addressing the identity-drift problem that caused AI video avatars to lose visual consistency over longer clips in earlier generations.

  • Identity drift: previous avatar models would gradually distort facial features and expression style over the course of a multi-minute video, making long-form content unreliable.
  • Avatar V maintains consistent identity across extended clips, unlocking production-length AI video for corporate training, marketing, and creator use cases.
  • HeyGen has been the leading AI avatar platform; this release widens the gap with competitors still struggling with consistency at scale.

  • SEVerA: Formally Verified Agents
