DeepSeek V4 ships 1M-token context as OpenAI releases GPT-5.5 and Codex goes autonomous

DeepSeek's V4 opens a million-token native context window at MIT-licensed open weights on the same day OpenAI ships GPT-5.5 to all paid subscribers and expands Codex into full software lifecycle automation. Alibaba's Qwen3.6-27B outperforms models 15x its size on coding benchmarks, and Anthropic publicly dissects three weeks of Claude Code quality degradation with specific dates, numbers, and a subscription reset for all affected users.

What matters today

DEEPSEEK V4

DeepSeek V4 ships with native 1 million-token context support at no price premium, releasing two open-weight variants under MIT license on April 24.

  • Two variants: V4-Pro (1.6T parameters, 49B active) and V4-Flash (284B parameters, 13B active).
  • At 1M-token length, V4-Pro requires 27% of V3.2's FLOPs and 10% of its KV cache; V4-Flash uses 10% of FLOPs and 7% of cache.
  • V4-Pro scores Codeforces rating 3,206 (23rd among human competitors) and 93.5% on LiveCodeBench.
  • Weights open-sourced on Hugging Face under MIT license.
  • Matches or exceeds Claude Sonnet 4.5 on agent tasks; falls marginally short of GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1-Pro on reasoning benchmarks.
  • Two Reddit threads generated 1,109 and 766 upvotes within hours of release, the highest engagement of any AI story today.

GPT-5.5

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23 to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise subscribers in ChatGPT and Codex, six weeks after GPT-5.4.

  • Excels at data analysis, code writing and debugging, software operation, online research, and document creation.
  • Greg Brockman stated GPT-5.5 can solve unclear problems with less user guidance.
  • API availability pending additional deployment safeguards.
  • HN thread drew 1,416 points and 945 comments, the highest engagement of any tech story on the site today.

ANTHROPIC POSTMORTEM

Anthropic published a postmortem identifying three separate changes between March and April 2026 that degraded Claude Code quality, with all issues fixed in v2.1.116 on April 20.

  • Reasoning effort downgrade (March 4-April 7): default shifted from high to medium to reduce latency, later reverted to xhigh for Opus 4.7 and high for other models.
  • Caching bug (March 26-April 10): thinking cache cleared every turn instead of once, causing Claude to appear forgetful and repeat itself.
  • Verbosity limit (April 16-20): an instruction capping text between tool calls at 25 words caused a measurable 3% drop in intelligence evaluations.
  • Anthropic reset usage limits for all subscribers on April 23.
  • Postmortem names specific dates, quantifies impact (3% intelligence drop), and acknowledges product optimization decisions directly degraded model quality.

QWEN3.6-27B

Alibaba released Qwen3.6-27B on April 22, an open-weight dense model that outperforms the larger Qwen3.6-397B MoE on agentic coding benchmarks.

  • 27B parameters; 262K-1.01M token context window.
  • Scores 77.2 on SWE-bench Verified and 59.3 on Terminal-Bench 2.0.
  • The 4-bit quantized version runs in 17GB of memory.
  • Architecture combines Gated DeltaNet linear attention with traditional self-attention.
  • Available under Apache 2.0 on Hugging Face and ModelScope; supports text, image, and video inputs.
  • HN thread scored 961 points with 437 comments.

OPENAI CODEX

  • April 2026 updates added macOS computer use, GitHub pull request inspection, Amazon Bedrock provider support, multi-environment app-server sessions, and deny-read sandboxing policies.
  • The model contributed diagnostics and test fixes to its own development during early versions.
  • Scheduled thread automations and artifact previews for PDFs, spreadsheets, and documents are now available.
  • Codex 3.0 launched on Product Hunt with 153 votes today.

Research worth watching

  • LLaDA2.0-Uni

    Unified discrete diffusion LLM combining multimodal understanding and generation. Achieves high-fidelity image generation comparable to specialized vision-language models. Most upvoted paper today (218 votes).

  • DR-Venus

    4B-parameter deep research agent trained on 10K open data points with agentic supervised fine-tuning and RL with turn-level rewards. Superior performance on research benchmarks at edge scale.

  • Reward Hacking in Large Models

    Demonstrates reward hacking in aligned LLMs stems from optimizing expressive policies against compressed reward signals, producing systematic misalignment. Direct relevance to RLHF-trained agentic systems.

  • TACO: Self-Evolving Terminal Agents

    Self-evolving compression framework discovering and refining context compression rules from interaction trajectories, reducing token overhead for long-horizon agent tasks.
