Sam Altman proposes a new social contract for ASI as Meta prepares to open-source its next model family

Sam Altman outlined a new framework for how OpenAI intends to govern the transition to superintelligence, framing it as a social contract rather than a technical milestone. Separately, Meta is preparing open-source releases of its next generation of models, continuing its strategy of matching proprietary labs on capability while keeping weights public. The New Yorker surfaced internal memos behind Sam Altman's 2023 firing, adding new detail to one of tech's most consequential governance crises.

What matters today

ALTMAN'S ASI SOCIAL CONTRACT

Sam Altman published a new framework for how OpenAI intends to approach artificial superintelligence, framing the company's obligations as a social contract with humanity rather than a set of technical safety constraints.

  • Marks a shift in how OpenAI publicly narrates AI governance: from safety protocols to a broader societal bargain.
  • Comes as OpenAI transitions from a capped-profit structure toward full commercial operation under investor scrutiny.
  • The framing is deliberate: "social contract" language positions OpenAI as a public interest actor rather than a private frontier lab.
  • Context: Altman's credibility on governance was dented by his 2023 firing and rapid reinstatement. The publication is part of an ongoing reframing effort.

META TO OPEN-SOURCE NEXT MODELS

Meta is preparing open-source releases of versions of its next generation of AI models, extending its strategy of publicly releasing competitive weights while proprietary labs tighten access.

  • Continues Meta's consistent pattern: release open weights after each generation to pull developer ecosystem toward its infrastructure.
  • Puts pressure on Anthropic and OpenAI, which have both tightened access to powerful models recently.
  • Wang's first Meta models are described as getting ready to ship.

ALTMAN FIRING MEMOS SURFACE

The New Yorker published an investigative report surfacing internal documents and memos behind Sam Altman's November 2023 firing from OpenAI, adding new detail to the board's reasoning.

  • Documents reveal previously undisclosed specifics about what the board cited in its decision.
  • Altman's reinstatement came within days after key investors and employees threatened to leave.
  • Raises unresolved governance questions about whether the board's original concerns were addressed or simply overruled.
