LiteLLM gets backdoored, Nvidia ships agent security infrastructure, and OpenAI winds down Sora

TeamPCP's supply chain attack on LiteLLM exposed 97 million monthly PyPI downloads to credential theft; Nvidia countered the week's security narrative at GTC 2026 with NemoClaw, an open-source privacy and sandboxing layer for autonomous agents; while OpenAI shut down the Sora video app six months after launch to redirect compute toward robotics world simulation, Figure AI debuted Figure 03 with reactive autonomy at a $39B valuation, and a developer proved a 400B parameter model can run entirely on an iPhone 17 Pro.

What matters today

LITELLM SUPPLY CHAIN ATTACK

Threat actor group TeamPCP compromised LiteLLM's CI/CD pipeline and pushed credential-stealing malware to PyPI, exposing 97 million monthly downloads for 2–3 hours on March 23.

  • Attack chain: Trivy CI/CD scanner compromised → maintainer credentials harvested → malicious versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 pushed to PyPI on March 23.
  • Malware installs a .pth file (litellm_init.pth) that executes on every Python startup, regardless of whether litellm is imported.
  • Three-stage payload with triple-layered base64 encoding and RSA-2048/AES-256-CBC encryption exfiltrates SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure credentials, Kubernetes configs, Docker configurations, crypto wallets, and environment variables.
  • 97 million monthly downloads affected; estimated 3 million daily users and tens of thousands of credential environments exposed.
  • Packages were live for 2–3 hours before quarantine. The Hacker News · Security Boulevard

NVIDIA NEMOCLAW

Nvidia released NemoClaw at GTC 2026, a software stack adding enterprise security to OpenClaw agents with single-command installation.

  • Installs Nvidia Nemotron models and OpenShell runtime; runs on GeForce RTX PCs, RTX PRO workstations, DGX Station, and DGX Spark.
  • Sandboxing includes process-level isolation, least-privilege access controls, and policy-based security guardrails for autonomous agents.
  • Privacy router uses differential privacy technology derived from Nvidia's Gretel acquisition to strip PII from prompts before sending to external frontier model APIs.
  • Jensen Huang framed agent trust as an infrastructure problem at GTC 2026 (March 16). TechCrunch · Futurum Group

OPENAI SORA SHUTDOWN

OpenAI announced the shutdown of the Sora app and API, citing compute costs and a strategic pivot toward robotics-focused world simulation research.

  • Sora 2 launched September 30, 2025 with an iOS app generating video up to 25 seconds with synchronized dialogue, sound effects, and music.
  • Videos included visible watermarks and invisible provenance signals; safety guardrails blocked sexual material, terrorist propaganda, and self-harm content.
  • Timeline from public product launch to discontinuation is under 6 months.
  • Compute reallocation targets world simulation for robotics, positioning physical AI as higher priority than consumer video generation.

FIGURE 03 HUMANOID ROBOT

Figure 03 debuted in a live demo with reactive autonomy, performing autonomous package sorting with real-time disruption recovery using end-to-end neural networks.

  • 5'6" tall, 30+ motors; slimmer hands with tactile finger pads and palm cameras for delicate manipulation.
  • 90% cheaper components than earlier Figure models; designed for warehouse logistics with reactive autonomy.
  • Figure AI raised $1 billion in September 2025 at a $39 billion valuation, backed by Nvidia, Microsoft, and OpenAI.
  • Targeting 2026 domestic deployment following partner testing. Time

IPHONE 17 PRO — 400B MODEL ON-DEVICE

A developer demonstrated Flash-MoE, a 400-billion parameter Mixture of Experts model, running entirely on-device on an iPhone 17 Pro with no internet connection.

  • Streams model weights from NVMe SSD to GPU on demand; model nominally requires 200GB in compressed form.
  • Activates less than 2% of weights per token — 4–10 of 512 experts — through selective expert routing.
  • Achieved 0.6 tokens per second with ~50 seconds time-to-first-token; proof-of-concept, not production-ready.
  • Builds on Apple's 2023 "LLM in a Flash" research. Full coverage

Research worth watching

  • Omni-WorldBench

    New benchmark for interactive 4D world models assessing temporal dynamics and causal interaction. Evaluates agents' ability to predict state transitions and plan multi-step actions. (107 votes)

  • Look Where It Matters / AwaRes

    Framework enabling VLMs to dynamically retrieve high-resolution image crops via tool-calling and multi-turn GRPO, cutting inference cost by processing only spatially relevant regions. (56 votes)

  • OpenResearcher

    Fully open pipeline for training deep research agents using offline search environments and synthesized long-horizon trajectories. Reproducible benchmark improvements on search-and-browse tasks. (50 votes)

  • RoboAlign

    Training framework using zero-shot natural language reasoning and RL to improve action accuracy in vision-language-action models, bridging language understanding with low-level robotic execution. (21 votes)
