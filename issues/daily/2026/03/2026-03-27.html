Anthropic's secret Mythos model leaks as voice AI ships from three directions at once

A CMS misconfiguration exposed Anthropic's unreleased Claude Mythos, rated by the company as an unprecedented cybersecurity risk; on the same day, Mistral shipped the first open-weights TTS model at production quality, Google rolled out Gemini 3.1 Flash Live to 200 countries, and Amazon rewired Polly for token-streaming pipelines, while Meta published TRIBE v2, a brain-encoding model trained on 700 fMRI subjects.

What matters today

ANTHROPIC MYTHOS LEAK

Anthropic accidentally exposed a draft blog post about Claude Mythos through a misconfigured CMS, revealing its most capable model to date and confirming limited early-access testing is underway.

  • Claude Mythos (also called Capybara internally) outperforms Claude Opus 4.6 by a substantial margin on coding, academic reasoning, and cybersecurity benchmarks.
  • Anthropic's own assessment describes Mythos as posing "unprecedented cybersecurity risks" and places it ahead of any other AI model in cyber capabilities.
  • The leak was caused by human error in CMS configuration, not an external breach; the exposed data was publicly searchable.
  • Early access is limited to a small group of customers as standard practice before broader release.
  • Anthropic confirmed the leak and acknowledged the exposure; no external breach occurred.

MISTRAL VOXTRAL TTS

Mistral Voxtral is a 4B-parameter open-weights TTS model, the first at production quality, available on Hugging Face under CC BY NC 4.0.

  • Supports 9 languages with voice cloning from 3 seconds of audio.
  • Benchmarks above ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 on naturalness with a 9.7x real-time factor.
  • Priced at $0.016 per 1,000 characters via API.
  • First open-weights TTS model at this quality tier, giving self-hosters a viable alternative to proprietary APIs.
  • TechCrunch covered the release.

GOOGLE GEMINI 3.1 FLASH LIVE

Google Gemini 3.1 Flash Live is Google's highest-quality audio model, with lower latency than 2.5 Flash Native Audio, now rolling out to 200+ countries.

  • Lower latency than 2.5 Flash Native Audio, doubled conversation memory length, improved background noise filtering.
  • Real-time conversation in 90+ languages.
  • Audio is watermarked by default.
  • Available via Gemini Live API; developers can build with it in Google AI Studio today.
  • Rolling out to 200+ countries.

AMAZON POLLY BIDIRECTIONAL STREAMING

Amazon Polly adds bidirectional streaming, letting applications send text and receive audio simultaneously over a single connection without waiting for complete sentences.

  • Designed specifically for LLM-generated text pipelines where output streams token-by-token, removing the sentence-boundary bottleneck.
  • AWS also added 10 new generative voices across 8 locales.
  • Expanded to London and Canada Central regions.
  • SDK support spans Java, JavaScript, .NET, C++, Go, Kotlin, PHP, Ruby, Rust, and Swift.

META TRIBE V2

Meta TRIBE v2 is a foundation model trained on 500+ hours of fMRI recordings from 700+ people that predicts human brain responses to video, audio, and text.

  • Achieves a 2-3x improvement over previous methods for movies and audiobooks.
  • Zero-shot generalization works on individuals the model has never seen.
  • Follows log-linear scaling laws with no performance plateau visible yet.
  • Training set of 500+ hours across 700+ subjects is an order of magnitude beyond prior brain encoding work.
  • Meta released weights, codebase, paper, and demo to the scientific community. The Tech Portal covered the release.

Research worth watching

  • Intern-S1-Pro: Scientific Multimodal Foundation Model at Trillion Scale

    A 1T-parameter model combining general and scientific reasoning with agent functionalities and reinforcement learning. State-of-the-art across multiple scientific disciplines. Points at what enterprise AI assistants will look like within the next model generation.

  • SlopCodeBench

    Coding agents fail on iterative long-horizon software tasks. Code quality degrades 2.2x faster than human code. Top agents solve only 17.2% of extended problems. A direct measurement of the gap between demo quality and production quality in coding agents.

  • MSA: Memory Sparse Attention for 100M Token Contexts

    Linear-complexity attention enabling 100M-token context windows without architectural changes. Practical for enterprise RAG over large document collections without proportional latency cost.

  • FinMCP-Bench

    Benchmark for LLM agents doing financial tool use under Model Context Protocol. Tests multi-step tool chaining at varying complexity levels in real-world financial domains.
