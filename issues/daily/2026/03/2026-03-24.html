Anthropic ships computer use, LiteLLM gets backdoored, and the enterprise agent land-grab accelerates

Anthropic turned its computer-use research into a shipping product for paying subscribers as LiteLLM's supply chain was compromised with a credential-stealing payload — while OpenAI, Nvidia, and Figure AI each pushed further into enterprise deployments, agent security infrastructure, and humanoid robotics production scale.

What matters today

ANTHROPIC COMPUTER USE

Anthropic launched Computer Use in research preview for Claude Pro and Max subscribers, enabling Claude to click, type, open apps, and navigate browsers on macOS.

  • Available to Claude Pro ($20/mo) and Max ($100/mo) subscribers in research preview.
  • Dispatch companion app lets users assign tasks from a phone while the desktop executes them asynchronously.
  • Architecture prioritizes existing service connectors (Slack, Google Calendar) before falling back to screen control, limiting blast radius.
  • Anthropic acquired computer-use startup Vercept in February; this product is the team's first public output.
  • Windows support is in pipeline.
  • Reddit thread logged 252 upvotes; Product Hunt #1 slot reached 404 votes on launch day.

LITELLM SUPPLY CHAIN ATTACK

LiteLLM versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 — a library with 95M monthly PyPI downloads — contained a credential stealer embedded in a .pth file that executes on Python startup regardless of whether LiteLLM is imported.

  • Attack attributed to the TeamPCP threat actor, likely via compromised PyPI credentials from an earlier Trivy exploit.
  • Payload targets SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure credentials, Kubernetes configs, database passwords, and crypto wallets.
  • Exfiltration uses AES-256-CBC + RSA-4096 encryption before sending to attacker infrastructure.
  • Any environment that ran versions 1.82.7 or 1.82.8 must treat all credentials as compromised.
  • Simon Willison's technical breakdown is the clearest public analysis available.

NVIDIA NEMOCLAW

Nvidia announced NemoClaw at GTC — an open-source enterprise agent platform with sandboxed execution, least-privilege access controls, and infrastructure-layer policy enforcement.

  • Built on OpenClaw; policy enforcement operates at the infrastructure layer, meaning compromised agents cannot reach it.
  • Developed with input from Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Security.
  • Deploys on cloud, on-premises, or local DGX/RTX hardware.

OPENAI FRONTIER ALLIANCES

OpenAI formalized Frontier Alliances — multi-year partnerships with McKinsey, BCG, Accenture, and Capgemini to deploy its Frontier agent platform inside Fortune 500 companies.

  • McKinsey and BCG own strategy engagements; Accenture and Capgemini own systems integration.
  • Early live deployments confirmed at Intuit, State Farm, Thermo Fisher, and Uber.
  • Anthropic holds parallel deals with Accenture and Deloitte — Accenture holds both relationships simultaneously.
  • Displacement risk for SaaS incumbents including Salesforce, Workday, and ServiceNow is accelerating through these consulting channels.

FIGURE 03 HUMANOID ROBOT

Figure AI unveiled Figure 03 — 9% lighter than its predecessor, with 2x faster actuators and tactile sensors detecting forces as small as 3 grams.

  • BotQ manufacturing facility targets 12,000 units/year initially, scaling to 100,000 over four years.
  • Figure 02 already contributed to 30,000 BMW vehicles as of November 2025.
  • CEO Brett Adcock's stated goal: robot-built robots within 24 months.
  • Reddit clip of the robot handling packages reached 1,715 upvotes.
