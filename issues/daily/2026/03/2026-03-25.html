March 25, 2026 Wednesday edition

OpenAI kills Sora and readies Spud as Anthropic, Mistral, and Apple expand their infrastructure positions

OpenAI is discontinuing Sora six months after launch and redirecting compute to its next model, Spud, while Anthropic commits $50B to U.S. data centers, Mistral launches Forge for training custom frontier models from scratch, Apple consolidates enterprise tools into a unified platform launching April 14, and Google sets a 2029 deadline for post-quantum cryptography migration across all systems.

What matters today

OPENAI KILLS SORA, SPUD NEXT

OpenAI is discontinuing Sora — the iOS app, API, and Sora.com — six months after launch, with freed compute redirected to Spud, its next major model family.

  • Sora's iOS app, API, and Sora.com all being discontinued — no shutdown date announced.
  • Disney $1B partnership wound down with no money changing hands.
  • Spud pretraining complete; rollout expected within weeks.
  • Sam Altman stepping back from safety oversight to focus on IPO fundraising and datacenter capacity.
  • Sora consumed significant compute under competitive pressure from Anthropic and Google.

ANTHROPIC $50B INFRASTRUCTURE

Anthropic is committing $50B to U.S. AI infrastructure, building data centers in Texas and New York with Fluidstack throughout 2026.

  • $50B total commitment, phased through 2026 with Fluidstack as infrastructure partner.
  • Initial data centers in Texas and New York, coming online throughout 2026.
  • 300,000+ business customers; large accounts up approximately 7x year-over-year.
  • 800 permanent jobs and 2,400 construction jobs created.
  • Aligns with Trump administration's AI Action Plan for domestic technology infrastructure.

MISTRAL FORGE

Mistral launched Forge at NVIDIA GTC, a platform that lets enterprises train fully custom AI models on proprietary data — training from scratch, not fine-tuning.

  • Forge trains custom models from scratch on proprietary data — distinct from fine-tuning approaches.
  • Announced at NVIDIA GTC 2026; early customers: ASML, Ericsson, and the European Space Agency.
  • Mistral Small 4 released alongside Forge.
  • Company projecting $1B+ ARR in 2026.

APPLE BUSINESS APRIL 14

Apple is merging Business Essentials, Business Manager, and Business Connect into a single unified platform launching April 14 in 200+ countries, with MDM now fully free.

  • Launches April 14, 2026 in 200+ countries and regions.
  • MDM/device management now free — previously paid under Business Essentials.
  • Business email, calendar, and directory with custom domain support included.
  • Blueprints feature enables zero-touch device deployment and preconfiguration.
  • Summer 2026 expansion adds Apple Maps advertising for business location promotion.
  • Premium storage starts at $0.99/month up to 2TB.

GOOGLE 2029 POST-QUANTUM DEADLINE

Google published an accelerated timeline targeting full post-quantum cryptography migration across all systems by 2029, warning that "store now, decrypt later" attacks are already underway.

  • 2029 target for full PQC migration across all Google systems.
  • ML-KEM already deployed for all internal Google traffic and Cloud KMS.
  • Android 17 ships with ML-DSA digital signature protection.
  • "Store now, decrypt later" attacks active now — data harvested today is at risk from future quantum computers.
  • Google recommends "crypto agility" — updating cryptographic systems without service disruption.
