OpenAI and Anthropic collide over Pentagon AI contracts as a federal judge steps in

A Pentagon blacklist of Anthropic and OpenAI's same-day competing defense deal set off internal backlash and a federal injunction, while the White House's new AI legislative blueprint fractured GOP unity in Congress. GitHub set an April 24 deadline for Copilot training opt-outs, Google shipped Stitch for free AI UI design, and Cohere released a 2B-parameter open-source transcription model that topped the HuggingFace ASR leaderboard.

What matters today

OPENAI, ANTHROPIC, PENTAGON

OpenAI announced a competing defense deal within hours of the Trump administration blacklisting Anthropic for refusing to sign a Pentagon contract without written prohibitions on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons.

  • Trump administration blacklisted Anthropic for refusing to sign a Pentagon contract without written prohibitions on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons.
  • OpenAI signed a competing Pentagon deal within hours of Anthropic's blacklisting.
  • Internal OpenAI backlash followed, with multiple employees publicly opposing the contract.
  • CEO Sam Altman privately told staff he had been trying to save Anthropic during the standoff, while in the same conversation criticizing Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei for years of attempts to undermine him.
  • Altman's approach used technical classifiers plus contractual language in place of Anthropic's written prohibition approach.
  • Altman noted that Claude was already embedded in government agencies.
  • A federal judge issued a preliminary injunction blocking the Pentagon ban on Anthropic.
  • Claude reached the Apple App Store top charts for the first time as users protested OpenAI's deal.

WHITE HOUSE AI FRAMEWORK

The White House released a national AI legislative blueprint on March 21 calling for federal preemption of state AI laws, fracturing GOP consensus in Congress.

  • Blueprint organized around six principles: child safety, energy costs, IP protection, anti-censorship, and public AI education.
  • Federal preemption would override California SB 1047 and dozens of state AI bills passed or pending.
  • GOP fracture centers on copyright language and how far federal authority should extend.
  • Democratic opposition: framework has no AI company accountability provisions.
  • White House seeking passage this year despite narrow House majority.
  • Sources: Axios, PBS NewsHour

GITHUB COPILOT TRAINING

GitHub set an April 24 deadline for Copilot Free, Pro, and Pro+ users to opt out of interaction data being used to train AI models.

  • Opt-out deadline: April 24, 2026 for Copilot Free, Pro, and Pro+ users.
  • Interaction data in scope: prompts, completions, code context, accepted and modified suggestions.
  • Private repository content at rest is not used for training.
  • Opt-out path: GitHub Privacy settings, "Allow GitHub to use my data for AI model training."
  • Copilot Business and Enterprise plan users are unaffected by this policy change.
  • Users who previously disabled data collection retain those preferences.

GOOGLE STITCH

Google shipped a major update to Stitch, its AI-native UI design tool, generating up to five interconnected screens from a text prompt and exporting working code to seven frameworks.

  • Generates up to 5 interconnected screens with shared design systems from a single text prompt.
  • Code export to 7 frameworks: HTML/CSS, Tailwind CSS, Vue.js, Angular, Flutter, and SwiftUI.
  • Brand guideline import for team-consistent output.
  • Simulation play function for testing user navigation flows.
  • Users select Gemini 2.5 Pro for quality or Gemini 2.5 Flash for speed.
  • Free, no paid tier. Additional details on the Google Developers Blog.

COHERE TRANSCRIBE

Cohere released cohere-transcribe-03-2026, a 2 billion parameter open-source speech recognition model under Apache 2.0 that tops the HuggingFace Open ASR Leaderboard.

  • 5.42% WER on HuggingFace Open ASR Leaderboard, ranked first.
  • Outperforms Whisper Large v3 (7.44%), ElevenLabs Scribe v2 (5.83%), and Qwen3-ASR (5.76%).
  • Supports 14 languages: English, German, French, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, Greek, Dutch, Polish, Arabic, Vietnamese, Mandarin, Japanese, Korean.
  • 2B parameter Conformer-based encoder-decoder trained on 0.5M hours of curated audio.
  • Apache 2.0 license; free API via Cohere; self-hostable on consumer-grade GPUs.
  • Coverage: TechCrunch
