May 5, 2026 Tuesday edition

OpenAI ships o4-mini-high as Google launches Gemini 3.2 Ultra and Nvidia unveils Blackwell Ultra cluster specs

OpenAI releases o4-mini-high with a 200K-token context window directly to all ChatGPT tiers while Google counters with Gemini 3.2 Ultra—its most capable multimodal model yet—available in AI Studio and Vertex. Nvidia's GTC keynote reveals Blackwell Ultra rack configurations exceeding 10 exaFLOPS per cluster, and Meta quietly open-sources Llama 4 Scout 2B, its smallest on-device model to date.

What matters today

OPENAI O4-MINI-HIGH

OpenAI released o4-mini-high today, a reasoning-optimized model with a 200K-token context window, rolling out to Plus, Pro, and Team subscribers immediately and to Enterprise by end of week.

  • Scores 91.2% on MATH-500 and 78.4% on GPQA Diamond, surpassing o3 on both benchmarks at one-third the compute cost.
  • Native tool use supports code execution, web search, and file analysis in a single turn with no prompt engineering required.
  • API access priced at $1.10 per million input tokens and $4.40 per million output tokens, undercutting o3 pricing by 40%.
  • HN thread reached 1,204 points and 712 comments within six hours of announcement.
  • Greg Brockman posted that o4-mini-high will power the next Codex autonomous coding sprint scheduled for May 12.

GEMINI 3.2 ULTRA

Google released Gemini 3.2 Ultra in AI Studio and Vertex AI, claiming top scores on video understanding, long-document reasoning, and multimodal code generation benchmarks.

  • 2M-token context window; native support for video, audio, images, code, and structured data in the same prompt.
  • Scores 87.6% on MMLU-Pro and achieves a new high of 68.3% on VideoMME, surpassing prior frontier results by 4.1 points.
  • Gemini 3.2 Flash and Flash-Lite variants released simultaneously; Flash is free-tier in AI Studio.
  • Google announces Deep Research integration with Google Workspace, beginning rollout to Business and Enterprise accounts this week.

NVIDIA BLACKWELL ULTRA

Nvidia's Jensen Huang unveiled Blackwell Ultra rack specifications at GTC, detailing configurations that deliver over 10 exaFLOPS of FP4 compute per 72-GPU rack.

  • Each GB300 Ultra GPU pairs 288GB HBM4 with NVLink 6.0 providing 1.8TB/s chip-to-chip bandwidth.
  • Microsoft, Google, and Oracle confirmed multi-billion-dollar orders; Amazon announced a dedicated Blackwell Ultra region in AWS US-East-3.
  • Full rack systems begin shipping to hyperscale customers in Q3 2026; cloud availability through NVIDIA DGX Cloud starts June 15.
  • Stock closed up 4.2% following the announcement.

META LLAMA 4 SCOUT 2B

Meta quietly released Llama 4 Scout 2B on Hugging Face under a permissive license, its smallest model tuned for on-device inference on smartphones and edge hardware.

  • 2B-parameter dense model runs at 40 tokens/second on an iPhone 16 Pro and under 2GB of RAM in 4-bit quantization.
  • Outperforms Gemma 3 2B on MMLU by 3.1 points and on HumanEval by 5.4 points.
  • Supports 32K-token context natively; extended context to 128K via chunked attention available as a flag.
  • HuggingFace page accumulated 14K downloads in the first four hours after release.

Research worth watching

  • AgentBench 3.0

    Comprehensive evaluation of 28 frontier models across 9 agentic task categories. Finds a persistent 22-point gap between top closed-weight and top open-weight models on long-horizon web tasks. Most upvoted paper today (341 votes).

  • SpecRouter

    Dynamic routing framework that selects among multiple speculative decoding heads at inference time, reducing latency by 38% on average with no accuracy loss across seven base models.

  • Chain-of-Verification for Tool Use

    Self-verification technique for agentic tool calls that catches 71% of hallucinated tool arguments before execution, cutting downstream task failure rates in half on ToolBench-Hard.

  • EfficientMoE-4

    Mixture-of-experts architecture that activates 3.2B parameters out of 64B total for typical inference while matching dense 13B models on quality benchmarks — closes the deployment gap for large MoE systems.
